The following Manx version of a poem by William Wordsworth is taken from a manuscript formerly in the possession of Dr. Clague of Castletown. It was given to the Library of the Manx Museum by the late Archdeacon Kewley (MS. 429/8A). The name of the translator is, unfortunately, not recorded, but his work is regarded as a fine example of nineteenth-century Manx Gaelic. Indeed it is a considerable improvement on the prosaic original of the famous poet. The latter is not printed here as it is readily available in Wordsworth's Collected Poems. The story of Harry Gill is based on an incident recorded by Erasmus Darwin, c. 1790 [Erasmus Darwin, ' Zoonomia,' 3rd edn. IV, 68.], to illustrate the effects of imagination. The farmer kept his bed for above twenty years for fear of the cold air, till at length he died.'",
